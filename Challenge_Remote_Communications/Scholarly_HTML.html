4. Remote communication application - Google Meet

Question 1. Observe the existing affordances, visual variables and interaction flows for a given use case â€“ for example, delivering an academic presentation or attending a specific meeting at late hours â€“ and propose better approaches (if any).

Answer 1. Let's consider the use case of delivering an academic presentation or attending a specific meeting at late hours in Google Meet. Existing affordances, visual variables, and interaction flows:

  1. Sharing screen: Google Meet provides the affordance of screen sharing, which allows the presenter to share their screen with the audience. The visual variable used for this affordance is a green icon with an upward arrow, which is located at the bottom of the screen. The interaction flow involves the presenter clicking on the icon and selecting the appropriate screen to share.
  2. Chat: Google Meet provides the affordance of chat, which allows participants to communicate with each other during the presentation/meeting. The visual variable used for this affordance is a speech bubble icon located at the bottom of the screen. The interaction flow involves the participant clicking on the icon and typing their message.
  3. Recording: Google Meet provides the affordance of recording, which allows the presenter to record the presentation/meeting. The visual variable used for this affordance is a red icon with a circle located at the bottom of the screen. The interaction flow involves the presenter clicking on the icon and selecting the option to record.

Proposed better approaches:

  1. Screen sharing: While screen sharing is a useful affordance, it can be improved by allowing the presenter to choose which specific application or window to share instead of sharing their entire screen. This can be achieved by providing a dropdown menu with a list of currently open applications/windows for the presenter to choose from.
  2. Chat: The chat affordance can be improved by providing the option to send private messages to specific participants instead of just sending messages to the entire group. This can be achieved by allowing the participant to click on the name of the participant they wish to message and opening a private chat window.
  3. Recording: While recording is a useful affordance, it can be improved by providing the option to automatically transcribe the recording. This can be achieved by using AI technology to transcribe the audio of the recording and provide a text-based version of the presentation/meeting.

Overall, by providing these proposed better approaches, Google Meet can enhance the user experience for delivering academic presentations or attending meetings at late hours.

Question 2. Compare the visual metaphors and idioms and suggest the similar ones for a text-only interaction (see CLI â€“ Command Line Interface).

Answer 2. Google Meet relies heavily on visual metaphors and idioms to convey information and functionality to users. For example, the "hand raise" icon or the "mute/unmute" button are visual metaphors that are easily recognizable and intuitive for users. However, when it comes to text-only interactions, such as a Command Line Interface (CLI), visual metaphors and idioms are not applicable. Instead, text-based idioms and language are used to convey functionality and commands.

Here are some examples of visual metaphors and idioms used in Google Meet and similar ones that can be used in a CLI:

    • Visual Metaphor: Hand raise icon
    • Text-based Idiom: "Request permission to speak"
    • In a CLI, a user can use a command such as "request permission to speak" to indicate they want to speak or ask a question. This can be achieved by implementing a feature that allows users to "raise their hand" using a specific command or keyword.

    • Visual Metaphor: Mute/unmute button
    • Text-based Idiom: "Toggle mute on/off"
    • In a CLI, a user can use a command such as "toggle mute on/off" to mute or unmute their microphone. This can be achieved by implementing a feature that allows users to use a specific command or keyword to mute or unmute their microphone.

    • Visual Metaphor: Share screen icon
    • Text-based Idiom: "Share screen with remote participants"
    • In a CLI, a user can use a command such as "share screen with remote participants" to share their screen during a call. This can be achieved by implementing a feature that allows users to use a specific command or keyword to share their screen with other participants.

Overall, while visual metaphors and idioms are not applicable in a text-only interaction such as a CLI, text-based idioms and language can be used to convey functionality and commands in a similar way. By implementing features that allow users to use specific commands or keywords, a CLI can be just as intuitive and easy to use as a visual interface like Google Meet.

Question 3. Discuss the cognitive overload in special conditions/contexts.

Answer 3. Cognitive overload occurs when a person's working memory is overwhelmed by too much information or too many tasks, leading to decreased performance and increased stress levels. In the context of Google Meet, cognitive overload can occur in several situations, including: Large group meetings: In large group meetings, participants may find it difficult to keep track of multiple speakers and discussions simultaneously. This can lead to cognitive overload as the brain struggles to process and remember all the information. Multitasking: During a Google Meet call, participants may also be multitasking, such as checking emails or working on other tasks while still participating in the call. This can lead to cognitive overload as the brain tries to switch between different tasks, leading to decreased performance in all tasks. Technical difficulties: Technical difficulties such as poor network connectivity or video/audio issues can also contribute to cognitive overload, as participants may struggle to hear or see what is happening in the call, leading to increased stress levels. To mitigate cognitive overload in these special conditions/contexts, Google Meet can take several steps, such as: Limiting the number of participants in a call: To reduce cognitive overload in large group meetings, Google Meet can limit the number of participants in a call or introduce features such as breakout rooms to allow for smaller group discussions. Encouraging focused attention: Google Meet can encourage participants to focus their attention on the call and limit multitasking by providing guidance on best practices and reminders to turn off distractions such as notifications or other tabs. Providing technical support: Google Meet can provide technical support to participants to reduce technical difficulties and prevent cognitive overload. This can include providing guidance on how to improve network connectivity or troubleshooting common video/audio issues. Overall, reducing cognitive overload in special conditions/contexts can improve the user experience and increase productivity in Google Meet calls.

Question 5. How the empiric laws (Fittsâ€™, Hickâ€™s, Steering) might be reconsidered/adapted in the context of multi-device interaction?

Answer 5. The empiric laws of Fitts', Hick's, and Steering can be reconsidered and adapted in the context of multi-device interaction in Google Meet as follows: Fitts' Law: Fitts' Law states that the time required to move a pointer to a target is a function of the distance to the target and the size of the target. In the context of multi-device interaction, this law can be adapted to account for the differences in the size and location of targets on different devices. For example, if a user is using a smartphone and a laptop simultaneously during a Google Meet call, the targets (such as buttons or links) on each device will be different in size and location. To accommodate this, the user interface can be designed to optimize the placement of targets on each device based on the device's screen size and orientation. Hick's Law: Hick's Law states that the time required to make a decision increases with the number of choices. In the context of multi-device interaction, this law can be adapted to account for the number of devices being used simultaneously. For example, if a user is using multiple devices during a Google Meet call, they may need to switch between devices to perform different tasks. To minimize the time required to make decisions, the user interface can be designed to provide clear and intuitive navigation between devices. Steering Law: The Steering Law states that the time required to move a pointer to a target is a function of the angle between the pointer and the target. In the context of multi-device interaction, this law can be adapted to account for the different angles and orientations of different devices. For example, if a user is using a smartphone and a laptop during a Google Meet call, the orientation of the devices may be different, and the user may need to adjust their movement to match the angle of the device. To accommodate this, the user interface can be designed to provide consistent and intuitive movement between devices, regardless of their orientation.

Question 6. List three new features that can be added to improve the experience and sketch those new features on paper and/or digitally.

Answer 6. Virtual Backgrounds: Google Meet should introduce a feature that allows users to change their backgrounds to a virtual image or video during a video call. This feature has already been introduced in other video conferencing apps and has become popular. It can help users maintain their privacy by hiding their actual location, and it can also add a fun element to video calls. Live Polls: Adding live polls during a Google Meet call can be a great way to engage participants and collect feedback from them. This feature can be useful for conducting surveys or gathering opinions on various topics discussed during the call. This feature can also provide real-time results, which can help in decision-making processes. Breakout Rooms: Google Meet should add the ability to create breakout rooms during a video call. This feature can be useful in group discussions where participants can be divided into smaller groups to have a more focused discussion. Participants can move between the rooms, and moderators can monitor the discussions in each room. This feature can be particularly helpful for online classes or virtual team meetings.

However, the keyboard navigation can be improved by providing a clear and logical order of focus on various elements of the interface, including buttons, menus, and controls. This can be achieved by following the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards for keyboard accessibility. Color Contrast: Google Meet's current color scheme may not be accessible for individuals with low vision or color blindness. Improvements can be made by increasing the contrast ratio between foreground and background colors, providing alternative text for images, and using clear and easy-to-read fonts. Additionally, a high-contrast mode can be provided for individuals who require it. Overall, these accessibility improvements can make Google Meet more inclusive and accessible for users with diverse needs. By implementing these changes, Google Meet can provide a better experience for all participants, regardless of their abilities or disabilities.
