1. Description

Envision a mobile application dedicated to groups of airsoft players that gather around in teams in an arena to play different airsoft matches. The user-interface will provide multi-language support for setting up the player teams, game type (for example, Deathmatch, Capture the Flag, SpeedSoft, Domination, 1:1), match duration and score, and other preferences. Moreover, the application would sync with the internal Bluetooth unit of the weapon replica and offer information such as ammo left, battery capacity and usage, plus other maintenance data. Also, the system would also sync with a smartwatch and a pair of augmented reality glasses to provide – by using various data visualization techniques – the contextual information for each player during a match.

  • The 4 (four) mandatory HTML5 persona documents, for various personas interacting to the system.
  • The HCI project documentation – as Scholarly HTML – regarding interaction design patterns used in the wireframes/prototypes. Discuss also the most aspects concerning the user flow, plus affective factors & emotion. Additionally, provide screen-captures explaining at least 2 storyboards/use-cases and a video demonstration – maximum 5 minutes, at (U)HD quality.

3. Introduction

During the second semester of the academic year 2022-2023 at the Faculty of Informatics Iasi, the Airsoft Companion project was successfully completed with contributions from Hriscu Alexandru-Gabriel, Anton Cosmin, and Bobu Dragoș. This section serves as an introduction to the project, providing an overview of its objectives and scope.

The Airsoft Companion project aimed to design and develop a mobile application specifically tailored for airsoft enthusiasts. The application aimed to enhance the airsoft gaming experience by providing features such as team management, equipment customization, match scheduling, and communication tools.

In order to meet the requirements of the project, various design and development tasks were undertaken. This included creating personas to understand the target audience and their needs, developing storyboards to visualize user interactions, designing user flows to define the navigation paths within the application, and creating sitemaps to outline the application's structure.

Sketches were also produced to ideate and explore different design concepts, while typography choices were carefully considered to ensure optimal readability and visual appeal. Color schemes were selected to create a cohesive and visually pleasing aesthetic, and contrast checkers were used to ensure accessibility for all users.

Wireframes were created to establish the layout and structure of the application's screens, and prototypes were developed to provide an interactive experience for user testing and feedback gathering. The design process also took into account affective factors and emotions to create an engaging and immersive experience for the users.

The use of design patterns, such as smartwatch patterns and conversational patterns, further enhanced the functionality and usability of the application across different platforms and interaction modes.

The subsequent sections of this report will delve into the specific aspects of the project, including personas, storyboards, user flows, sitemaps, sketches, typography, color schemes, contrast checkers, wireframes, prototypes, affective factors & emotion, design patterns for web/desktop/mobile, smartwatch patterns, conversational patterns, voice patterns, AR/VR patterns, video demonstration, team members, conclusion, and references. Each section will provide detailed insights and findings related to the respective topic.

The following section will focus on personas, providing a comprehensive understanding of the target users and their characteristics within the context of the Airsoft Companion application.

4. Target Audience

The target audience for AiCo (Airsoft Companion) primarily consists of airsoft players and enthusiasts. These are individuals who actively participate in airsoft games, organize or join teams, and engage in competitive or recreational airsoft activities. The target audience may include both experienced players who are familiar with the sport and its intricacies, as well as newcomers who are seeking guidance and support in navigating the world of airsoft. AiCo aims to cater to players of all skill levels, providing them with a comprehensive and user-friendly mobile application that enhances their airsoft experience. Additionally, AiCo may also attract individuals who are curious about airsoft and want to learn more about the sport, its rules, equipment, and community.

5. Personas

In accordance with the requirement, we have created the four mandatory HTML5 persona documents that represent various personas interacting with the system. These persona documents serve as valuable resources for understanding and empathizing with the target users of the Airsoft application. Each persona document provides detailed insights into the profiles, backgrounds, goals, frustrations, scenarios and use cases of the respective user persona.

By developing these persona documents, we have aimed to ensure a user-centered approach in the design and development of the Airsoft application. These documents serve as reference points throughout the design process, enabling the team to make informed decisions based on user needs and preferences. The persona documents also help in identifying potential pain points, designing relevant features, and tailoring the user experience to meet the diverse requirements of the different personas.

Overall, the inclusion of these persona documents reinforces the importance of user-centric design and contributes to creating an Airsoft application that effectively caters to the needs and expectations of its target users.

10. Typography

In the implementation of the user interface, a sans-serif font was utilized, following a typography scale of 3:4 for consistent font sizes. Additionally, a line spacing of 1.25 was applied to enhance readability and legibility throughout the design.

11. Color schemes

For the Airsoft color scheme, a split-complementary approach was chosen, incorporating three colors with the following distribution: 60% primary color, 30% secondary color, and 10% tertiary color. This color scheme combines a primary color with two colors that are adjacent to its complement, resulting in a balanced and visually appealing palette.

For the split-complementary color scheme within Airsoft, here are the three colors and their hex codes:

  1. Primary Color (60%):
    • Hex: #FF0000
    • Description: Red
  2. Secondary Color (30%):
    • Hex: #0080FF
    • Description: Blue
  3. Tertiary Color (10%):
    • Hex: #00FF80
    • Description: Emerald Green

12. Contrast Checkers

In the Contrast Checker Validation section for Airsoft, We have conducted the following assessments, involving overlay colors and combinations with white and black backgrounds:

  1. Overlay Colors:

    We have tested the contrast ratio of overlay colors used in Airsoft to ensure readability and accessibility. By overlaying text or elements on different background colors, we have verified that the contrast ratio between the overlay and background meets the recommended accessibility standards.

  2. Combination with White/Black Backgrounds:

    Additionally, we have examined the contrast ratio when the Airsoft interface is displayed on white and black backgrounds. This assessment ensures that the content remains legible and visually distinct, providing optimal readability and user experience in varying lighting conditions.

By conducting these contrast checker validations, we have aimed to prioritize accessibility and ensure that the Airsoft application maintains clear visibility and readability for all users, regardless of their visual capabilities or environmental conditions.

16. Affective factors & emotion

Affective factors & emotion play a crucial role in designing a user-centered experience for the Airsoft application. We have carefully considered these factors throughout the design process to create a more engaging and satisfying user experience.

Firstly, we have incorporated appropriate color schemes and typography choices that align with the emotional tone we want to evoke. The color scheme we selected consists of vibrant and energetic colors, such as #FF0000 (red), #0080FF (blue), and #00FF80 (emerald green), which are known to stimulate excitement and enthusiasm among users. These colors are strategically used to create a sense of adrenaline and intensity, reflecting the spirit of the Airsoft experience.

Furthermore, we have paid attention to the use of visual elements and graphics that evoke emotions related to teamwork, competition, and thrill. The wireframes and prototypes include dynamic imagery showcasing players in action, capturing the excitement and camaraderie of the Airsoft gameplay. Additionally, the user flow incorporates elements that promote a sense of achievement and progress, such as progress indicators and rewards, to heighten the emotional engagement of the users.

In terms of interaction design, we have focused on providing responsive and intuitive feedback to user actions. For example, when a user successfully completes a task or achieves a milestone, the application responds with celebratory animations or positive messages, reinforcing a sense of accomplishment and boosting user satisfaction. Conversely, when errors occur, clear error messages are displayed along with helpful instructions, ensuring that users feel supported and guided throughout their journey.

Moreover, we have taken into consideration the emotional impact of microinteractions. Smooth transitions, subtle animations, and haptic feedback have been incorporated to provide a delightful and immersive experience, creating a positive emotional connection between the user and the application.

Overall, by considering affective factors and emotions, we have strived to create an Airsoft application that not only meets functional requirements but also elicits an emotional response from users, enhancing their overall satisfaction and enjoyment.

    • Center Stage: Center the main content of the application on the screen to grab the user's attention and focus them on the key information.
    • Toolbars: Provide toolbars to offer quick access to frequently used commands and actions.
    • Sidebars: Use sidebars to display additional information, settings, or secondary navigation.
    • Panels: Implement panels to organize and display different sets of information or functionalities in a flexible manner.
    • Widgets: Add widgets or interactive components to provide additional functionality and information.
  3. Actions and Commands:
    • Trackpad Actions: Allow users to use trackpad gestures such as pinch, swipe, and tap to perform actions like zooming, navigating, or selecting.

Mobile Design Patterns for AiCo

For the Airsoft Mobile application, the following patterns would be ideal to implement:

  1. Navigation:
    • Top bar: Use a top navigation bar to display elements such as the application logo, page title, and navigation options.
    • Bottom bar: Implement a bottom navigation bar to display common actions and quick navigation options.
    • Floating button: Add a floating button to provide a quick and easily accessible action, such as creating a new match or adding a member to a team.
    • Dashboard: Use a dashboard to provide an overview of relevant information, such as upcoming matches, scores, and statistics.
  2. Layout:
    • Cards: Use cards to display information about teams, players, and matches in an attractive and organized visual format.
    • Tiles: Implement tiles to present various options and functionalities of the application in an easily accessible and navigable manner.
    • Grids: Use grids to arrange and display information in an orderly and structured way.
  3. Actions and Commands:
    • Tap: Use the tap gesture to select items or open pages and details.
    • Swipe: Allow users to perform swipe gestures to navigate between pages or perform specific actions, such as deleting an item.
    • Long press: Implement the long press gesture to provide additional options for an item, such as editing or sharing it.
    • Pinch, spread, pan, drag: Use these gestures to allow users to perform actions like zooming, panning, or dragging on a map or image.
    • Swipe up-down-left-right: Enable users to perform swipe gestures in different directions to navigate or scroll the page content.
    • Shake: Use the shake gesture to trigger an action or reset certain settings.
  4. Tables and Lists:
    • Horizontal tables: Use horizontal tables to display comparative information about players, teams, or matches.
    • Tables as lists: Present information in the form of lists with rows and columns to allow users to explore and sort the data.
    • Cards alternative: Replace the traditional tabular structure with alternative cards to provide a more visual and interactive view of the data.
  5. Form Patterns:
    • Label: Using labels to describe each input field and indicate to the user what information needs to be provided in that field.
    • Input: Using appropriate input fields for specific data types, such as text fields for names, email addresses, URLs, etc. For multiple selections, use radio buttons, checkboxes, or select fields.
    • Placeholder: Adding suggestive texts in input fields to indicate the expected type or format of the data.
    • Errors, Warnings, Success: Providing error messages, warnings, and success confirmations to communicate the outcome of the user's action.
    • Hover, Active, Visited: Adding visual effects for user interaction, such as highlighting fields when selected or hovering over an element.
    • Inputs: Using specific input types for data such as date/time, slider, toggle, textarea, number, file, multiple selection, colors, rating, location, etc.
    • Progress Bar: Having a multi-step form completion flow and using a progress bar to indicate to the user which stage they are at.
    • Form Wizard: For more complex forms, using a step-by-step wizard to guide the user through the form completion process.
    • Dedicated on-screen keyboards: Using dedicated on-screen keyboards for certain data types, such as numeric keyboards for number inputs or special keyboards for email addresses or URLs.
